# Shapes and geometry
M2 provides native 2D circles, edges/chains, capsules, boxes, convex polygons and monotone-chain hull construction, compounds, heightfields and half-spaces; and native 3D spheres, edges, capsules, boxes, enumerated convex hull construction, compounds, triangle meshes, regular-grid heightfields and half-spaces. Convex shapes expose support mappings. Finite shapes expose local AABBs, origin-relative bounding radii, containment where they enclose volume, closest points, ray tests and validated scaling. `transform_aabb2/3` produces world bounds.

Non-uniform scaling is accepted only for boxes. Other finite shapes require positive uniform scale. Half-spaces are infinite and therefore intentionally have no finite AABB, bounding sphere, or mass. Edges, open meshes and heightfield surfaces have no solid mass; closed meshes provide signed-tetrahedron mass. Compound mass uses the parallel-axis theorem. Capsule3 inertia decomposes cylinder plus paired hemispheres with an explicitly documented engineering approximation. Convex-hull volume/centroid is signed-tetrahedral; its diagonal inertia is vertex-lumped numerical integration. Mesh diagonal inertia uses deterministic tetra-centroid quadrature. Use authored mass overrides when high-accuracy arbitrary-mesh inertia is required.

Hull building is a deterministic O(n⁴) reference implementation that enumerates supporting triples, rejects coplanar point sets and removes duplicate points. Mesh BVHs use stable median splits and leaves of at most four triangles. Current closest/ray mesh queries retain exact full-triangle reference traversal while the BVH is exposed and ready for M3 accelerated mid-phase traversal.
